Scoring methodology

← Profile Report
The canonical, versioned contract behind every number on your profile: the research anchors, the bands, the formulas, what's validated vs experimental — and how it evolves. No percentiles, no cohorts; measurements only.

Where the data comes from

Every signal is read locally from your own AI coding tools and git — far more than the first-class tools. Each source declares a fidelity tier: deep (real sessions parsed) · counts (countable artifacts) · presence (install only, usage marked insufficient). Counts and presence never move a score.

The same six dimensions also drive archetypes, kinds, and positioning — all from counted signals. No model ever writes a score.

What weighs how much

The composite is a weighted average of the six dimensions. Pick how you build to watch the weights adapt — then click any dimension for the signals and the research behind it.

Bars are colored by evidence strength: research anchored weights rest on cited studies; reasoned default weights are chosen constants — openly versioned, not yet validated. The heaviest weights track the strongest evidence — the reasoning behind each is in the thesis and the studies in the references. Click a bar for the signals and the research behind it.

How every number is derived

Each number's exact logic, our reasoning for it, and an honest label for the kind of claim it is. Measured = a count, true by definition · research-anchored = grounded in a cited study · reasoned-default = a chosen constant, openly versioned, not yet validated · estimate = a labeled band. The six dimensions are detailed in the explorer above.
